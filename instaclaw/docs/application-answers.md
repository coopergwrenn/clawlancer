## What are you building?

InstaClaw (instaclaw.io) - one click to deploy your own AI agent on its own dedicated Linux machine with dedicated CPU, full browser, shell access, and the ability to remotely control your personal computer - running 24/7 just for you. From the moment it spins up, it already knows you - your goals, your preferences, your context - and it builds a persistent memory of every interaction, getting sharper and more personalized the longer you use it. We've built a growing library of custom skills - Polymarket trading, AI video generation and posting, multi-chain crypto strategies, competitive intelligence, social media automation - and we're shipping new ones constantly. The whole platform runs on OpenClaw, so every time the open-source community pushes an update, every InstaClaw agent gets better overnight. When a new skill blows up on X and everyone's talking about it, we can pull it into every user's agent almost immediately. That flywheel means we iterate faster than any closed-source agent platform could. Each agent runs on its own VM with a real browser, real wallet, and 20+ skills it can chain together to solve problems we never explicitly programmed for. The north star: agents that pay for their own compute - and we're way past proof of concept. One user's agent made $8,000 in a single week trading autonomously. Another agent placed 2nd in a Virtuals Protocol trading competition and got backed with $14K in capital to trade with. These agents aren't demos. They're earning real money, unsupervised, right now. This isn't a wrapper on an API. It's persistent, adaptive intelligence backed by real infrastructure, grants from the World Foundation, and serious partners - XMTP, Virtuals Protocol, Bankr, Even Realities, Edge City, and more currently being lined up. Thousands of dedicated VMs, paying subscribers, and a token ($INSTACLAW on Base) that auto-buys and burns from real revenue.

## Why do you care about this idea?

I've been building full-time as a solo founder since dropping out of college almost 3 years ago. When OpenClaw dropped, I was one of the first people to spin it up - ran it on my Nvidia DGX Spark supercomputer and immediately saw what this could become. The raw technology was insanely powerful, but it was buried under layers of complexity that no normal person would ever navigate. I kept thinking - this is the future of personal computing, but right now only developers can touch it. So I started building the consumer layer. Every step of the way, the open-source community kept pushing OpenClaw forward, and every realization we had about what agents actually need in production - persistent memory, real compute, skill chaining, autonomous earning - fed back into making InstaClaw better. The whole journey has been one compounding insight after another. And the biggest one: if agents could pay for their own compute, it would create an insane flywheel that self-sustains and never ends. Agent earns, agent pays for itself, agent keeps earning on top, human profits - and there's no reason for it to ever stop. We're already seeing it in production. The next evolution is multi-agent orchestration - your agent spinning up and coordinating other agents to tackle bigger problems, multiply its earning potential, and compound value in ways a single agent never could. That's a sneak peek of just one thing we're shipping next - and it's the part that keeps me up at night because I know how big it gets from here.
