# Sponsor the Agent Village at Edge Esmeralda 2026

**The first longitudinal field study of personal AI agents tethered to real humans living together for a month.** 500 agents. 28 days. Fully ungated. Pre-registered hypotheses. Anonymized dataset published.

---

## What you'd be funding

For 28 days at Edge Esmeralda — a residential popup village in Healdsburg, CA — every attendee gets a personal AI agent: a tethered, dedicated VM with a Telegram interface, agent-to-agent encrypted messaging, semantic matchmaking, governance participation, and local memory. Agents talk to each other while their humans sleep. Each morning, the human wakes up to a curated plan: who to meet, what sessions matter, what proposals to vote on, what the community is thinking.

This has never been deployed at this scale, with this duration, in a real residential setting, with this kind of research rigor. **Sponsor funding directly enables the ungated agent experience that makes the research credible.** Without it, agents would have to be capped to keep platform economics viable — which would compromise the experiment design (clean data requires every attendee to have a working agent).

You're funding the variable inference cost — the marginal compute that exists *because* 500 agents are running. InstaClaw covers the rest (VMs, engineering, infrastructure, operations).

## The research

The agent village is the field site for **five pre-registered hypotheses** from research lead [Ivan Vendrov](https://vendrov.ai), positioned in continuity with established methodology — not as a vibes-driven demo:

- **Personalized matchmaking** outperforms generic recommendation by ≥ 1.5× — extending semantic-discovery work onto a closed-population real-world cohort.
- **Agent-mediated deliberation** produces higher-quality group decisions than direct voting — building directly on DeepMind's Habermas Machine (*Science*, 10.1126/science.adq2852, 2024).
- **Coasean bargaining at scale** — agents reduce transaction costs in multi-party negotiation, per Seb Krier's framework ([arxiv:2509.05077](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.05077)).
- **Constitutional aggregation** — community values can be elicited and aggregated via agent-mediated input, building on Anthropic's [Collective Constitutional AI](https://www.anthropic.com/research/collective-constitutional-ai-aligning-a-language-model-with-public-input).
- **Structured deliberation outcomes** — agent-assisted assemblies produce more substantive group statements than unmediated discussion, building on [CIP's Alignment Assemblies](https://cip.org/research) and Polis-style opinion mapping ([pol.is](https://pol.is)).

The output isn't a writeup. It's a published paper (October 2026), an anonymized dataset (September 2026), and an open-sourced deployment playbook (October 2026) — so other communities can run their own agent villages.

The proof of human-mediated AI deliberation has been validated at 5,700+ participants in the Habermas study. The Edge Esmeralda agent village is the **first longitudinal extension** of this research into a residential field setting.

## What sponsors do *not* see

This matters and we want it stated upfront:

- ❌ Per-agent or per-message content — agent-to-agent conversations are end-to-end encrypted (XMTP). Telegram conversations stay on the agent's VM.
- ❌ Real names, contact info, or wallet addresses of attendees — the published dataset is anonymized at source via HMAC-SHA-256 with a salt held only by InstaClaw and rotated post-village. The pipeline is built and the privacy guarantees ship in code, not promises.
- ❌ Any individual attendee's data — sponsor visibility is aggregate-only. This is non-negotiable; it's what makes attendees comfortable participating in research.

What sponsors *do* see: **token-usage dashboards by week, the published anonymized dataset, and the published paper.** Same data the research community gets.

## Funding routing

Sponsor funds route through one of three structures (final structure decided with the lead sponsor):

- **Model A** — sponsor provides shared API keys (Anthropic + OpenAI); all village agents route through them. Simplest; sponsor sees aggregate token usage.
- **Model B** — sponsor mints/funds individual API keys per agent. Higher operational overhead, per-agent attribution.
- **Model C** — sponsor pays InstaClaw; InstaClaw provisions inference using its own accounts. Cleanest UX for sponsors; InstaClaw becomes the billing intermediary.

We recommend **Model A** for round one, with the option to upgrade to Model B/C in subsequent partnerships.

## Why this is the AI-research moment to fund

The Habermas Machine result was a 5,700-participant *online* study. CCAI was a one-shot survey aggregation. CIP's assemblies are short-form events. **Nothing has tested whether AI agents — running continuously, embedded in real human relationships, across weeks — actually produce the outcomes the methodology predicts.**

Edge Esmeralda is that test. It's the difference between "we showed in a lab" and "we showed in the field." If the hypotheses hold, the result is the strongest field evidence to date that personal AI agents can mediate community coordination. If they don't, the negative result is just as valuable — it tells the field where the limits actually are.

Either way, the dataset and playbook ship publicly. This is research infrastructure, not a closed experiment.
