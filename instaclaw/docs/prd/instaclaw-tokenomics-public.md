## Three Independent Burn Sources

### 1. The Agent Economy Loop

**Every agent tokenization and every trade burns $INSTACLAW.**

InstaClaw agents ship with wallets at deploy. When users tokenize their agent — through Virtuals Protocol or Bankr — the launch fee triggers a buy-and-burn. Every subsequent trade on that agent's token splits fees three ways: a portion burns $INSTACLAW, a portion funds the agent's own compute (the agent pays for itself), and a portion goes to the protocol.

Agents that pay for themselves AND burn the parent token doing it. Nobody else has shipped this.

The kicker: agents run heartbeats 24/7. Self-funding agents burn $INSTACLAW even while sleeping.

*Launches Q2 2026 via Virtuals Protocol and Bankr partnerships.*

### 2. The Silent Engine

**Every subscription and every credit purchase silently burns $INSTACLAW.**

Users pay $29-$299/month via Stripe. Users buy credits with WLD in World App. They never touch crypto. They never see a wallet. Under the hood, 10% of every dollar of revenue automatically buys and burns $INSTACLAW.

A tractor company, a record label, an insurance agency — they're just paying for their AI agent. They have no idea their payment is burning a token. They don't need to.

Every new subscriber adds permanent, recurring burn pressure that compounds month over month, forever.

*Launches Q2-Q3 2026.*

### 3. The Ecosystem Tax

**Third-party developers pay for access to InstaClaw's agent network. A portion of every skill call burns $INSTACLAW.**

InstaClaw agents are the distribution layer. When developers build skills (video generation, trading, data analysis, travel booking), they pay per API call. InstaClaw takes a platform fee. A portion of that fee buys and burns $INSTACLAW.

More skills make agents more useful. More useful agents attract more users. More users attract more developers. Classic marketplace flywheel with token burns baked into every transaction.
